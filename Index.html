Tool 01

Talent Pool Quantifier

Before you open a req, know what you're walking into. Estimate the available pool, gauge sourcing complexity, and get a concrete search strategy — in seconds.

Sample output — VP of Supply Chain · Riyadh, KSA
Director / VP Challenging FMCG / Retail

Estimated pool

600–1,200

Active candidates in market

Search difficulty

Challenging

Niche + senior + local pref

Est. time to fill

10–16 wks

With a proactive strategy

Sourcing strategy

  • Lead with LinkedIn Recruiter — use Boolean: ("VP Supply Chain" OR "Supply Chain Director") AND ("FMCG" OR "CPG" OR "Retail") AND "Saudi Arabia"
  • Map competitors: Almarai, Savola, PepsiCo KSA, Unilever Arabia, Nestlé Gulf — extract VP-level profiles directly
  • Tap CSCMP and APICS Gulf chapters for passive senior talent; post in the SCM Saudi Arabia LinkedIn group
  • Prioritise bilingual (Arabic/English) candidates with Vision 2030-aligned experience; Saudisation (Nitaqat) compliance is essential for this role
  • Referral play: ask your current supply chain contacts in the region — senior TA networks in KSA are tight-knit

Key risks

Counter-offer risk is high at this level — VP candidates in KSA are actively headhunted. Expect 20–30% of offers to face a competing offer. Additionally, the relocation reluctance of non-KSA candidates (if open to expats) will narrow your pool further.

Quick wins to start today

  • Run a LinkedIn talent insights report for "VP Supply Chain" in KSA — share it with the hiring manager to align on market reality
  • Identify 15–20 target profiles today and send personalised InMails referencing a specific project or achievement from their profile

Tool 02

Intake Meeting Prep

Walk into every hiring manager kickoff meeting fully prepared. Get a tailored set of smart questions so you get the brief right the first time — and never leave a meeting without everything you need.

Sample output — Senior Data Engineer · Series B Tech · Dubai
Technology Critical urgency Hiring Manager: CTO

Opening (set the tone)

"Thanks for making time — I want to make sure I'm fully equipped to go to market for you with confidence. The goal for today is to align on what great looks like, agree on the process, and flag anything that could slow us down. I'll take notes and send you a summary so we're both accountable to the same brief."

Role clarity questions

  • If this person is exceptional, what does their first 90 days look like — what will they have built or delivered?
  • How does this role connect to your current engineering roadmap, and what's the biggest gap it closes?
  • Is this a new headcount or a backfill? If backfill — what happened with the previous person and what do you want to do differently?
  • What does success look like at the 6-month and 12-month mark?

Red flags to surface

  • Is there an internal candidate in consideration? I want to make sure we're running a fair and efficient process.
  • Has this role been open before? If so, what happened — did we get close and lose someone, or did the search stall?
  • Is the compensation range approved and competitive for the UAE market? I can share a benchmark if useful.

Tool 03

Candidate Scorecard Builder

Give your interview panel a structured, bias-aware scorecard so every candidate is assessed against the same criteria — consistently, fairly, and defensibly.

Sample output — Regional Sales Manager · Mid-market SaaS
Manager level Competency-based 3 interview stages

Scoring scale

1 = Poor | 2 = Below bar | 3 = Meets bar | 4 = Strong | 5 = Exceptional

Competency: Commercial acumen

What we're assessing: Ability to identify revenue opportunities, manage pipeline discipline, and translate customer needs into commercial outcomes.

Score of 5 looks like: Can articulate how they've built territory revenue from scratch, demonstrates strong pipeline hygiene, and gives specific examples of closing complex multi-stakeholder deals.
Score of 1–2 looks like: Vague on numbers, no clear ownership of their pipeline, or attributes success entirely to the product rather than their own sales process.

Dealbreakers checklist

  • No prior SaaS or recurring revenue experience (mandatory)
  • Cannot demonstrate consistent quota attainment over 2+ years
  • Has never managed or influenced a multi-stakeholder sales cycle
  • Unwilling to travel within the region (role requirement)
